Description of about Chantry Island

Chantry Island is a glacial moraine and consists of stone above the water and beneath extending a mile north and a mile south of the island. These underwater shoals of massive granite boulders have made this area one of the most treacherous in the great lakes. There are over 50 known shipwrecks around the island and there are many accounts from the 1800s and early to mid 1900s of these disasters and lost lives. Today buoys and modern navigational tools safely guide boats through the area.
